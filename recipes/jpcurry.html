Japanese Curry recipe

Craving comfort? Dive into Japanese curry, a sweet and savory hug in a bowl. Unlike its spicy cousins, this roux-coated wonder blends mild spices and fluffy rice, warming hearts as easily as stomachs. Born from British influence, it's uniquely Japanese, a staple on family tables for generations. Why? It's simple, affordable, endlessly customizable, and brimming with umami goodness. From meaty delights to veggie feasts, there's a curry for every mood. So, grab your spoon and join the Japanese tradition of curry love - one savory bite at a time.

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil, or more as needed
  • 1 ¾ pounds beef chuck, cut into 2-inch cubes
  • 3 onions, quartered
  • 1 tablespoon ketchup
  • 1 ½ teaspoons Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 pinch cayenne pepper, or to taste (Optional)
  • water to cover
  • 4 carrots, cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 1 cube chicken bouillon (Optional)
  • 3 medium potatoes, cut into 3-inch chunks
  • 1 ½ (3.5 ounce) containers Japanese curry roux, or more to taste

Steps

  1. Heat oil in a 6-quart pot over medium-high heat. Pour in water to cover mixture by 1 or 2 inches. Add carrots and bouillon.
  2. Add beef and saute until brown, 5 to 7 minutes.
  3. Add onions and cook until starting to soften, about 3 minutes.
  4. Add ketchup and Worcestershire sauce and stir to coat.
  5. Add cayenne pepper.
  6. Pour in water to cover mixture by 1 or 2 inches and add carrots and bouillon.
  7. Simmer, skimming fat off the surface of the broth as needed, for 30 minutes.
  8. Add potatoes.
  9. Stir in 1 package of curry roux and let dissolve; add remaining curry as needed to achieve desired thickness.
  10. Continue simmering until beef and vegetables are tender, about 30 minutes more.
